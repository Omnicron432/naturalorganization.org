This paper introduces the Branching Sensitivity Measure — a calculable metric that quantifies the extreme sensitivity of expected species diversity to the effective branching rate λ. This suppression is precisely the predicted signature of the M/I/C triad acting as an internal governor from the operational floor. 1. Introduction

For over a century, the neo-Darwinian synthesis has treated random mutation plus natural selection as sufficient to explain the origin and diversification of biological form. Yet observations of deep morphological stasis (horseshoe crab body plans conserved for ~450 million years), limited extant diversity (~8–10 million species), and categorical organizational biases at the sequence level stand in tension with this claim.

This tension crystallized in a specific insight: if variation and selection were unconstrained, the numbers should explode. The Branching Sensitivity Measure operationalizes that insight into a reproducible, quantitative test.

The question is not whether natural selection occurs — it does. The question is whether random variation plus selection constitute a sufficient explanation for the pattern of life observed, including both its diversity and its stasis. The Branching Sensitivity Measure provides a calculable answer.

3.2 The Critical Threshold

The critical value p ≈ 6.07 × 10⁻¹³ is required to match observed diversity (~10⁷ species). A single order of magnitude increase — to 6.07 × 10⁻¹² — produces extinction. A single order of magnitude above the critical value — 6.07 × 10⁻¹¹ — produces an explosion to >>10³⁰⁰ expected species.

What this means in plain terms:

The knife-edge precision required — a branching probability calibrated to within one order of magnitude out of thirteen — is not a property of random variation plus selection. Random variation would explore all values of p. Selection has nothing to calibrate before Φ > 0 is already operative.

The observed pattern of life sits precisely on the narrow ridge between extinction and explosion. That precision is the signature of an intrinsic organizing principle — the M/I/C governor — not of unconstrained stochastic search.

3.3 Mass Extinctions Cannot Close the Gap

Mass extinctions contribute only a ~(0.25)⁵ ≈ 10⁻³ multiplicative factor to Nfinal. This is three orders of magnitude of reduction. The gap between the critical threshold and any biologically plausible p above it spans dozens to hundreds of orders of magnitude. Five mass extinctions reducing diversity by 75% each time cannot close a gap of that size. The suppression must be intrinsic and continuous — not episodic.

6. Darwinian Sufficiency and Temporal Closure

The Branching Sensitivity Measure satisfies Darwin's own falsification condition on Darwin's terms. Darwin wrote that his theory would break down if a complex organ could be demonstrated to be impossible to form by numerous successive slight modifications. The M/I/C triad satisfies this — but the Branching Sensitivity Measure satisfies a related and perhaps stronger condition:

If random variation plus natural selection were unconstrained, the number of species that should exist exceeds 10³⁰⁰. The observed number is ~10⁷. The gap between these numbers — more than 293 orders of magnitude under even modestly higher branching probabilities — is not explained by selection pressure, mass extinctions, or ecological carrying capacity. It requires an intrinsic governor that operates before selection, constraining which variations are generated in the first place.

This governor is the M/I/C triad. Its presence at the molecular level is confirmed by the Anchor-Single data. Its required magnitude is calculated by the Branching Sensitivity Measure. The two results are consistent and mutually reinforcing.

Geological time does not help. The argument is not that there hasn't been enough time. The argument is that more time produces more branching events, each of which — without intrinsic suppression — contributes to the explosion. Geological time makes the problem larger, not smaller, in the absence of the M/I/C governor.

The Branching Sensitivity Measure provides a calculable demonstration that the observed pattern of biological diversity and stasis is not an anomaly but the predicted outcome of the Coordination Protocol Φ > 0 with its M/I/C minimum as an internal governor.

Random variation plus natural selection, unconstrained, produces an explosion of diversity exceeding 10³⁰⁰ expected species at any biologically plausible branching probability above 6.07 × 10⁻¹². The observed ~10⁷ species requires that the effective branching probability be suppressed by 8–12 orders of magnitude relative to raw mutation rates. This suppression is the M/I/C governor — measured at the molecular level by the Anchor-Single data, required at the macroscopic level by the Branching Sensitivity Measure, and predicted by the Generalized Existence Theorem.

The insight has been formalized, modeled, and integrated into the Generalized Existence Theorem, closing the final quantitative gap in the argument for constitutive intelligence at the foundation of persistent systems.
